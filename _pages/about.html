  • 1994

    I was born in a city called Aleppo located near the northern Syrian border. Aleppo is regarded as one of the oldest inhabited cities in the world dating back to 12000 BCE.

  • 2001

    I started attending the American college for boys at the age of 12. I finished my middle school and high school there. Right around a young age, I had my mind set on becoming a computer engineer. It was always cool to be the kid who fixes laptops, download games and music to phones XD, help the family around with their simple technology demands. I used to sit for hours going through the dial-up internet connection we had back then to search new games, new music and cool knowledge; like how old retro games are made. In high school I had a huge interest in games and animes, that was when I created my first form to share some content and fan made translations and arts.

  • 2012

    I started my University life at Aleppo University, Computer Science department around 2012. I was young, full of energy and passion eager to discover more about the techonlogy world. Right around that time, the Syrian revolution started taking a new turn as more and more cities became more involved. As one can expect, it became very hard to sustain an academic life.

  • 2014

    As I finished 2 years in my pursue for a bachelor's degree at the University of Aleppo, the Syrian crisis turned into a full-fledged war. As much as it pained me to leave, I had to try and pursue another path to carry on. I was granted a full scholarship through the YTP Turkish Scholarship program to study Computer Science at Ataturk University. I had many hardships and lots and lots of great memories. It was challenging and fun to learn a new language from scratch and adapt new life and culture.

  • 2019

    My undergrad life experience came to an end in 2019. I graduate second in my class. I had a lot of fun, made a lot of unbreakable bonds and learned a lot about computer architecture, data structures and algorithms, web and app development, microprocessors and embedded systems and my favorite subjects; computer vision and machine learning. Perhaps my highlight moment for my undergraduate life in Erzurum, Turkey is that I met the love of my life there.

  • 2015

    As part of our university requirements, I took my internship at a fun and friendly workplace. I got trained with a team of students and they eventually became my friends. There were so many fun activities and parties here.

  • 2016

    Graduation Day!

  • 2017

    I worked at Cloudstaff, the company where I took my internship. I was part of the Mobile Development Team. I wrote apps, maintained the codebases, added new features.

  • 2018

    I got the chance to work with awesome people from USA. We worked together to build the core products of Bucket. I was the iOS Developer on the team. I also helped the Android guys whenever there was a chance.
